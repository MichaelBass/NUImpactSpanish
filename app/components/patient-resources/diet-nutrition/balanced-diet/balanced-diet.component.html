Maintaining a Balanced Diet

Escuche el audio completo de esta página a continuación

Eating a well-balanced diet can help you feel better and maintain a healthy weight. Here are tips on how to maintain a balanced diet.



Portion Control


Much of the problem of overweight and obesity in this country is due to portion sizes that are too big! It is important to watch your weight because obesity is linked to heart disease, diabetes, stroke, and some cancers.


  • Meats, Fish, and Poultry = deck of cards or upper side of hand when clenched in fist..
  • Fats (butter, salad dressings, sour cream, etc.) = 1 tablespoon
  • Bread = 1 slice
  • Pasta = 1 ounce or ½ cup cooked.
  • Cereal = 3/4 to 1 cup

Food Labels

Food labels are your guide on the road to healthy eating.

Labels can:

  • Help you figure out how many nutrients you are getting in your daily diet
  • Help you compare the nutrients in specific products so you can make the choices that are best for you.

Things to pay attention to on food label:

  • Serving Size:

    • This tells you how big a serving is. Is it 1 cup? Is it 3 crackers? Is it 2 oz.?

  • Servings Per Container:

    • This mean how many servings are in each package. So, if there are two servings and you eat the whole package, you must multiply the calories, fat, etc. by 2 to see how many calories,fat, etc. you have eaten!

  • Calories:

    • This is how many calories there are in each serving.A calorie is a unit of energy—or what we consume every day through eating and drinking.Calories are important because eating too many calories leads to weight gain.

The amount of calories you need each day depends on many factors like your

  • Risk of cancer
  • Heart disease
  • Diabetes
  • Stroke

Weight gain may lead to increased:

  • Height
  • Weight
  • Gender
  • Current activity level.

We recommend that you talk to your doctor about a daily recommended calorie intake to lose, maintain, or gain weight.

  • Total Fat:

    • This is how many grams of fat there are for each serving (not in the whole package). You want no more than 3 grams of fat for every 100 calories.

  • Sodium:

    • Doesn’t matter too much for weight loss, but for hypertension, it does.Daily maximum – 2,400 mg
